Research Overview

About Me

Koo Yoon-mo

Graduate Student / Researcher

Koo Yoon-mo received a B.S. degree in Electronics Engineering from Myongji University in 2025. He is currently pursuing a graduate degree in the Department of Photonics and Nanoelectronics at Hanyang University, Ansan, Republic of Korea.

His current research focuses on advanced packaging technologies for next-generation displays. Specifically, he is investigating Through-Glass Via (TGV) interposers for Micro LED integration, developing precise self-assembly methods using micro-fluidics, and exploring novel fabrication techniques for high-aspect-ratio microstructures.

Research Interests

Stretchable & Flexible Electronics

Development of deformable electronic systems using wavy interconnects and island-bridge structures. Research on flexible substrates and conductive materials to realize next-generation wearable devices and skin-attachable sensors that maintain performance under mechanical strain.

TGV Metallization & Solder Interface

Advanced metallization processes for Through-Glass Via (TGV) substrates to enable high-density 3D packaging. Investigating the interfacial reactions between solder materials and electrode pads to enhance the electrical and mechanical reliability of micro-joints in ultra-fine pitch packaging.

Micro-structure Design for FSA

Design and fabrication of functional microstructures using SU-8 and other polymers. Focusing on optimizing the geometry of receptors and capture sites to maximize the yield and orientation accuracy of chips during the Fluidic Self-Assembly (FSA) process.

Micro LED Transfer Process

Optimization of massively parallel transfer technologies for Micro LEDs. Developing Fluidic Self-Assembly (FSA) protocols to achieve high-speed, high-yield placement of microscopic LED chips onto large-area display substrates, overcoming the limitations of conventional pick-and-place methods.
